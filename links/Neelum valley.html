Neelum Valley:

A Hidden Gem in Azad Kashmir Located in the Azad Kashmir region of Pakistan, Neelum Valley is a breathtakingly beautiful destination that attracts tourists from all over the world. The valley is situated in the north-east of Muzaffarabad, the capital city of Azad Kashmir, and is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and unique history.

Geography and Climate

Neelum Valley is a long, narrow valley that stretches for approximately 200 kilometers. The valley is surrounded by the majestic Himalayan mountain range, including the famous Nanga Parbat peak, which rises to an altitude of 8,125 meters. The climate of Neelum Valley is cold and temperate, with long winters and short summers. The valley receives heavy rainfall and snowfall during the winter months, and the soil is fertile and suitable for agriculture.

History and Culture

Neelum Valley has a rich and unique cultural heritage. The valley has been inhabited by the Kashmiri people for centuries, and the local culture is a blend of Kashmiri, Pakistani, and Indian influences. The valley has a strong tradition of music, dance, and handicrafts, and the local people are known for their hospitality and warm welcome to tourists.

Things to Do

Neelum Valley offers a wide range of activities for tourists, including:
1. Trekking:
The valley has many trekking routes, including the famous Neelum Valley Trek, which offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
2. Hiking:
There are many hiking trails in the valley that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
3. Fishing:
The valley has many rivers and streams that are home to a wide variety of fish, including trout and mahseer.
4. Boating:
The valley has many lakes and rivers that offer boating opportunities, including the famous Neelum River.
5. Cultural Tours:
The valley has a rich cultural heritage, and there are many cultural tours that offer a glimpse into the history and traditions of the local people.

Getting There

Neelum Valley is accessible by road and air. The valley is connected to the rest of Pakistan by the Neelum Valley Road, which is a scenic and well-maintained road that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. The nearest airport is the Muzaffarabad Airport, which is located approximately 50 kilometers from Neelum Valley. Accommodation Neelum Valley has a wide range of accommodation options, including hotels, guesthouses, and campsites. The valley has many luxury hotels that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains, as well as budget-friendly guesthouses and campsites.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Neelum Valley is from May to October, when the weather is warm and sunny. The valley is accessible by road during this time, and the scenic views of the surrounding mountains are at their best. However, the valley is also beautiful during the winter months, when the snow-capped mountains and frozen lakes offer a unique and breathtakingly beautiful landscape.
